The Garki Model [1] has seven compartments:

  • Uninfected, non-immune (\(x_0\))

  • Exposed and incubating, non-immune (\(x_1\))

  • Infectious positive (\(y_1\))

  • Non-infectious positive (\(y_2\))

  • Uninfected, immune (\(x_3\))

  • Exposed, incubating, and immune (\(x_4\))

  • Non-infectious positive recovering fast (\(y_3\))

The Motivating ODE

Time to clear here is \(R_i(h) = \frac{h}{e^{h/r_i}-1}\)

The differential equations are:

\[\begin{equation}\begin{array}{rl} \dot x_1 &= -h x_1 + R_1(h) y_2 \\ \dot x_2 &= h x_1 - \nu x_2 \\ \dot y_1 &= \nu x_2 - \alpha_1 y_1 \\ \dot y_2 &= \alpha_1 y_1 - \left( R_1(h) + \alpha_2 \right) y_2 \\ \dot y_3 &= \alpha_2 y_2 + \nu x_4 - R_2(h) y_2 \\ \dot x_3 &= R_2(h) y_2 - h x_3 \\ \dot x_4 &= h x_3 - \nu x_4 \end{array}\end{equation}\]

A solver exists in the RAMP xDE Model Library

The Probability Transition Matrix

The assumption that parasites clear at the rate \(R_i(h) = \frac{h}{e^{h/r_i}-1}\) creates problems for RAMP-TML because any translation of the formula would be nonlinear in the attack rate \(\alpha\), which would break the peek-ahead method. Since we know that at \(\alpha =0\), the fraction remaining infected would be \(e^{-r_i\tau}\) and that at \(\alpha = 1\), all would remain infected, we simply interpolate, so that the fraction clearing is \(e^{-r_i \tau} + \alpha_t (1-e^{-r_i \tau}).\)

We translate the daily transition rates into \(\tau\)-day transition proportions in the following way:

  • \(x_1 \rightarrow x_2\) and \(x_3 \rightarrow x_4\) at rate \(h\). Let \(A = 1-e^{-h \tau}.\)

  • \(x_2 \rightarrow y_1\) and \(x_4 \rightarrow y_3\) at rate \(\nu\). Here, we take \(\tau=10\) and we assume all infections transition in one time step.

  • \(y_1 \rightarrow y_2\) at rate \(\alpha_1\). Let \(v_3 = 1-e^{-\alpha_1 \tau}.\)

  • \(y_2 \rightarrow x_3\) at rate \(q_4 = \alpha_t + (1-\alpha_t) exp^{-r_1 \tau}.\)

  • \(y_2 \rightarrow y_3\) at rate \(\alpha_2\). Let \(v_4 = 1-e^{-\alpha_2 \tau}.\)

  • \(y_3 \rightarrow x_3\) at rate \(q_6 = \alpha_t + (1-\alpha_t) exp^{-r_1 \tau}.\)

The probability transition matrix is the following:

\[\begin{equation}\begin{array}{c|ccccccc} & x_1 & x_2 & y_1 & y_2 & y_3 & x_3 & x_4 \\ \hline x_1 &1-A& . & . & q_4 & . & . & . \\ x_2 & A& . & . & . & . & . & . \\ y_1 & . & 1 & 1-v_3 & . & . & . & . \\ y_2 & . & . & v_3 & 1-v_4-q_4 & . & . & . \\ y_3 & . & . & . & v_4 & 1-q_6 & . & 1 \\ x_3 & . & . & . & . & q_6 & 1-A & . \\ x_4 & . & . & . & . & . & A & . \\ \end{array}\end{equation}\]

Rh.garki = function(alpha, r=1/200, tau=10){
  h = -log(1-alpha)/tau
  1-ifelse(alpha==0, exp(-r*tau), exp(-h*tau/(exp(h/r)-1)))  
}

Rh.tml= function(alpha, r=1/200, tau=10){
  1-alpha - (1-alpha)*exp(-r*tau)
}

Rh = Rh.tml
constructP_garki = function(alpha, par){with(par,{
  h = -log(1-alpha)/tau
  q4 = Rh(h,r1,tau)
  q6 = Rh(h,r2,tau)
  t(matrix(c(
  c(1-alpha, 0,0,q4,0,0,0), 
  c(alpha, 0,0,0,0,0,0), 
  c(0, 1,1-v3,0,0,0,0), 
  c(0, 0,v3,1-v4-q4,0,0,0), 
  c(0,0,0,v4,1-q6,0,1), 
  c(0,0,0,0,q6,1-alpha,0), 
  c(0,0,0,0,0,alpha,0)), 7, 7)) 
})}
decomposeP_garki= function(alpha,par){
  P0 =  constructP_garki(0,par)
  if(alpha==0){ 
    list(P0 = P0, P1 = 0*P0)
  } else{
    P1 = (constructP_garki(alpha,par) - P0)/alpha
    list(P0 = P0, P1 = P1)
  }
}
makeParams_garki = function(alpha1 = 0.002, alpha2 = 0.00019, 
                            r1 = .00023, r2 = 0.0023, 
                            q1 = 1, q2=1, q3=.7, tau=10){
  v3 = 1-exp(-alpha1*tau)
  v4 = 1-exp(-alpha2*tau)
  calDlm =  c(0,0,q1,q2,q3,0,0)
  calDtrue = c(0,0,1,1,1,0,0)
  lmSens = function(X){
    sum(calDlm*X)/sum(calDtrue*X)
  }
  X2x = function(X){
    sum(calDlm*X)
  }
  imm = function(X){
    sum(c(0,0,0,0,1,1,1)*X)
  }
  list(v3=v3,v4=v4,r1=r1,r2=r2,tau=tau, calDlm = calDlm, calDtrue=calDtrue,X2x=X2x,lmSens=lmSens,imm=imm)
}
par = makeParams_garki()
Pgarki = constructP_garki(.01,par)

makeX0_garki = function(par){
  c(1, rep(0,6))
}

makeXx_garki = function(y=c(0.1,0.1),par){
  c(1-y[1]-y[2], 0, 0, y[1],0,y[2],0,0) 
}
PtimesX_garki = function(X, alpha, par){
  calP = constructP_garki(alpha, par)
  return(calP%*%X) 
}

For the Garki Model, the steady state is not a point but a curve describing the epidemiological state by age.

ar2steadyState_garki = function(alpha, par, tMax = 365){
  X = makeX0_garki(par)
  Xt = X  
  for(i in 1:tMax){
    X = PtimesX_garki(X, alpha, par)
    Xt = cbind(Xt, X)
  }
  return(Xt)
}
Xt = ar2steadyState_garki(.06, par) 
x = t(as.matrix(par$calDlm)) %*% as.matrix(Xt)
plot(c(0:365)/36.5, x, type = "l", xlab = "Age (Years)", ylab = "Prevalence")

arCohort_garki = function(alpha, par, tMax=1460*2, plotIt=F){with(par,{
  X = makeX0_garki(par)
  x = X2x(X) 
  sens = lmSens(X)
  pIm = imm(X)
  for(age in 1:tMax){
    X = PtimesX_garki(X, alpha, par)
    x = c(x, X2x(X))
    sens = c(sens, lmSens(X)) 
    pIm = c(pIm, imm(X)) 
  }
  cohort = list(x=x, sens=sens, pIm=pIm, ageY = c(0:tMax)*tau/365) 
  if(plotIt==TRUE) plotCohort_garki(cohort)
  return(cohort) 
})}
plotCohort_garki = function(cohort){with(cohort,{
  plot(ageY, x, type = "l", ylim = c(0,1), xlab = "Age (Years)")
  lines(ageY, sens, col = "blue")
  lines(ageY, pIm, col = "green")
})}

plotx_garki= function(cohort){with(cohort,{
  plot(ageY, x, type = "l", ylim = c(0,1), xlab = "Age (Years)")
})}

linex_garki= function(cohort){with(cohort,{
  lines(ageY, x)
})}
